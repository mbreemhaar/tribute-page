The Eagles

Members

Don Henley

Donald Hugh Henley (born July 22, 1947) is one of the founding members of the Eagles. He is the drummer and co-lead vocalist. Henley sang the lead vocals on Eagles hits such as “Witchy Woman”, “Desperado”, “Best of My Love” and, of course, “Hotel California”.

Glenn Frey

Glenn Lewis Frey (November 6, 1948 - January 18, 2016) was one of the co-founding members of the Eagles. He was mainly a singer and rhythm guitarist, but he also played the piano on some records. Frey sang the lead vocals on “Take It Easy”, “Peaceful Easy Feeling”, “Tequila Sunrise” and many more other Eagles songs.

Bernie Leadon

Bernard Matthew Leadon (born July 19, 1947) is one of the founding members of the Eagles. Leadon is a muli-instrumentalist and plays guitar, banjo, mandolin, steel guitar and dobro. Leadon left the Eagles in 1975 after which Joe Walsh took his place.

Randy Meisner

Randall Herman Meisner (born March 8, 1946) is one of the founding members of the Eagles. He was the bassist of the band from 1971 until 1977, after which Timothy B. Schmit took his place. Meisner was lead vocalist for the hit song Take It To The Limit.

Joe Walsh

Joe Walsh (born November 20, 1947) joined the Eagles in 1975, taking Bernie Leadon's place. Like Leadon, Walsh is a multi-instrumentalist and plays keyboards and guitar. Walsh also sings the main vocals on songs like In The City and Pretty Maids All in a Row.
